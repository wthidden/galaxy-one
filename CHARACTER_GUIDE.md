Choose your destiny among the stars. Each character type offers a unique path to victory with special abilities, strategic advantages, and distinct playstyles.

## üè≠ Empire Builder - The Master of Industry

### "Through industry and infrastructure, we shall build a civilization that endures for millennia."

### Background

The Empire Builders are visionaries who see the galaxy not as a battlefield, but as a canvas for civilization. They are master engineers, city planners, and economic architects who believe that true power comes from sustainable growth, efficient production, and technological advancement. Where others see barren worlds, Empire Builders see potential‚Äîworlds waiting to be transformed into thriving industrial centers.

**Philosophy:** Progress through production. Victory through economic supremacy.

**Famous Quote:** *"Give me enough industry and I will reshape the galaxy."*

### Special Abilities

**Industrial Efficiency:**
- Build industry at **4 industry + 4 metal** (others pay 5 + 5)
- Increase population limits at **4 industry + 4 metal** (others pay 5 + 5)
- 20% cost reduction on all infrastructure

**Starting Advantages:**
- Begin with extra industry on homeworld
- Natural economic growth bonus
- Efficient resource utilization

### Gameplay Strategy

**Early Game (Turns 1-10):**
1. **Maximize Production**
   - Use `W1I2I` to build industry early
   - Increase population limits with `W1I3L`
   - Focus on infrastructure over military

2. **Economic Foundation**
   - Build industry first, ships second
   - Each industry point generates long-term value
   - Think compound interest‚Äîinvest early, profit later

**Mid Game (Turns 11-25):**
3. **Industrial Snowball**
   - Convert economic advantage into fleet production
   - Use `W1B50F5` to mass-produce ships
   - Expand to resource-rich worlds

4. **Infrastructure Network**
   - Build industry on conquered worlds immediately
   - Maintain population limits ahead of growth
   - Create self-sustaining colonies

**Late Game (Turns 26+):**
5. **Economic Dominance**
   - Outproduce all opponents combined
   - Replace losses faster than enemies can inflict them
   - Overwhelm through sheer numbers

### Tips & Tactics

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Build industry EARLY‚Äîevery turn counts
- Increase population limits before they cap out
- Maintain 2:1 ratio of industry to ships
- Use migration to fill high-limit worlds
- Reinvest profits into more infrastructure

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Rush military production too early
- Ignore population limit increases
- Build ships before you have the industry to support losses
- Waste your cost advantage on frivolous builds

### Strengths
- **Unmatched Economy:** Outproduce everyone in late game
- **Sustainable Growth:** Can replace losses efficiently
- **Long-term Power:** Grows stronger every turn
- **Flexibility:** Economic base supports any strategy

### Weaknesses
- **Slow Start:** Takes time to build economic advantage
- **Early Vulnerability:** Weaker military in first 10 turns
- **Investment Heavy:** Must sacrifice early aggression for infrastructure
- **Tempting Target:** Rich worlds attract raiders

### Win Condition
**Economic Superiority:** Achieve victory by building an unstoppable industrial machine that churns out fleets faster than enemies can destroy them.

**Typical Victory Score:** First to 8000+ through infrastructure and territory

## üí∞ Merchant - The Trade Prince

### "In the void between worlds, profit flows to those who control the trade routes."

### Background

Merchants are the galaxy's economic elite‚Äîshrewd traders, master negotiators, and logistics experts who understand that wealth is the ultimate power. They don't just trade goods; they trade influence, information, and favors. With their massive cargo haulers and economic networks, Merchants can move populations and resources faster than anyone else, turning economic pressure into political power.

**Philosophy:** Capital conquers where armies cannot. Trade routes are more powerful than battleships.

**Famous Quote:** *"Why conquer worlds when you can buy them? Why fight wars when you can fund them?"*

### Special Abilities

**Cargo Mastery:**
- **2x Cargo Capacity:** Carry 2 population per ship (others carry 1)
- Can move populations twice as fast
- Economic warfare through migration

**Consumer Goods:**
- Use `F5N10` to unload cargo as consumer goods
- Boosts world productivity
- Increases population growth rate

**Starting Advantages:**
- Extra cargo capacity from turn 1
- Superior population mobility
- Economic flexibility

### Gameplay Strategy

**Early Game (Turns 1-10):**
1. **Establish Trade Routes**
   - Use `F5L` to load maximum cargo (2 per ship!)
   - Move populations to strategic worlds
   - Build economic networks early

2. **Population Redistribution**
   - Empty weak worlds, fill strong ones
   - Concentrate population at high-limit colonies
   - Use `F5N` to boost productivity with consumer goods

**Mid Game (Turns 11-25):**
3. **Economic Warfare**
   - Drain enemy worlds through migration
   - Flood your worlds with population
   - Use cargo capacity to outmaneuver opponents

4. **Strategic Relocation**
   - Move populations away from danger
   - Rapidly colonize newly conquered worlds
   - Create population centers that fuel massive builds

**Late Game (Turns 26+):**
5. **Trade Empire**
   - Control critical migration routes
   - Use loader permissions (L=player) for alliances
   - Deny cargo access (X=player) to enemies
   - Dominate through economic manipulation

### Tips & Tactics

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Load 2 population per ship‚Äîexploit your advantage!
- Use consumer goods to boost world productivity
- Build large cargo fleets (50+ ships)
- Control migration chokepoints
- Offer loader status to allies for favors
- Drain enemy worlds before attacking

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Build small cargo fleets‚Äîgo big or go home
- Waste your cargo advantage on small moves
- Leave valuable populations on weak worlds
- Ignore consumer goods mechanic
- Give loader status to untrustworthy players

### Strengths
- **Population Mobility:** Move people 2x faster than anyone
- **Economic Flexibility:** Rapidly shift resources
- **Diplomatic Tool:** Loader status builds alliances
- **Population Warfare:** Drain enemy productivity

### Weaknesses
- **Combat Mediocre:** No direct combat bonuses
- **Requires Planning:** Cargo advantage needs strategy
- **Cargo Vulnerability:** Loaded fleets are tempting targets
- **Complex Management:** More moving pieces to track

### Win Condition
**Economic Control:** Dominate through superior population distribution, economic warfare, and trade route control.

**Typical Victory Score:** Win through massive population centers and economic choke points

## üè¥‚Äç‚ò†Ô∏è Pirate - The Scourge of Space

### "The galaxy belongs to those bold enough to take it. No mercy. No surrender."

### Background

Pirates are raiders, marauders, and opportunists who thrive in chaos. They don't build‚Äîthey take. They don't negotiate‚Äîthey plunder. Where others see civilizations, Pirates see targets. Where others see alliances, Pirates see weakness to exploit. They are the terror of the spaceways, striking without warning and vanishing into the void with their spoils.

**Philosophy:** Why build when you can steal? Why farm when you can raid?

**Famous Quote:** *"Your industry is my industry. Your population is my metal. Your mistake was building where I could find you."*

### Special Abilities

**Plunder:**
- Use `W10X` to plunder enemy worlds
- Converts population directly to metal
- Devastates enemy economy
- No diplomatic penalty

**Raiding Specialist:**
- Bonus to hit-and-run tactics
- Can plunder without owning world (with fleet present)
- Terrorize enemies without conquest

**Starting Advantages:**
- Begin with raiding fleet
- Plunder ability from turn 1
- Aggressive expansion benefits

### Gameplay Strategy

**Early Game (Turns 1-10):**
1. **Raid & Pillage**
   - Find rich worlds with `F5Y10` (probe)
   - Move in with `F5W10`
   - Plunder with `W10X`‚Äîturn their population into YOUR metal!

2. **Hit and Run**
   - Strike vulnerable worlds
   - Plunder and retreat before reinforcements arrive
   - Use speed and aggression

**Mid Game (Turns 11-25):**
3. **Economic Warfare**
   - Plunder converts population to metal (1:1)
   - Deny enemies their population growth
   - Use stolen metal to build your fleet

4. **Terror Tactics**
   - Keep enemies defensive and afraid
   - Raid multiple targets to spread their forces thin
   - Plunder worlds just before they grow

**Late Game (Turns 26+):**
5. **Pirate King**
   - Control key worlds through fear
   - Use plunder to maintain massive fleet
   - Strike deals: "Pay tribute or be raided"
   - Profit from chaos

### Tips & Tactics

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Probe before you plunder‚Äîknowledge is power
- Plunder worlds with high population
- Use mobility to avoid retaliation
- Target players who invest in population
- Plunder just before turn end (deny regrowth)
- Keep fleets mobile and ready to raid

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Plunder worlds you want to keep productive
- Stay in one place too long
- Ignore military strength
- Plunder worlds with low population (not worth it)
- Make yourself predictable

### Strengths
- **Economic Disruption:** Devastate enemy economies
- **Metal Generation:** Turn population into metal instantly
- **Psychological Warfare:** Keep enemies afraid
- **Low Maintenance:** Don't need to build infrastructure

### Weaknesses
- **No Economic Base:** Must constantly raid
- **Diplomatic Isolation:** Everyone hates Pirates
- **Unsustainable:** Can't plunder forever
- **Requires Targets:** Need enemies with population

### Win Condition
**Reign of Terror:** Win through relentless raiding, economic disruption, and preventing others from building advantage.

**Typical Victory Score:** Win by keeping everyone else weak, not by being strongest

## üîÆ Artifact Collector - The Seeker of Power

### "Ancient technologies hold the keys to ultimate power. I will find them all."

### Background

Artifact Collectors are archaeologists, treasure hunters, and wielders of ancient power. They seek the legendary artifacts scattered across the galaxy‚Äîrelics of a long-dead civilization that offer unimaginable advantages. While others fight over metal and industry, Artifact Collectors pursue something far more valuable: the secrets of the ancients.

**Philosophy:** Knowledge is power. Ancient power is absolute.

**Famous Quote:** *"You fight with ships. I fight with the weapons of gods."*

### Special Abilities

**Artifact Mastery:**
- Artifacts provide massive bonuses (combat, economy, special powers)
- Can sense artifact locations
- Bonuses stack with multiple artifacts
- Artifacts cannot be destroyed

**Ancient Technology:**
- Some artifacts grant unique abilities
- Combat multipliers
- Economic bonuses
- Special movement or visibility powers

**Starting Advantages:**
- Begin with knowledge of nearby artifacts
- Natural advantage in artifact hunts
- Bonus to artifact discovery

### Gameplay Strategy

**Early Game (Turns 1-10):**
1. **Artifact Rush**
   - Explore aggressively with `F5Y10` (probe)
   - Search for artifact worlds
   - Race opponents to discoveries
   - Use `V5F1` to check artifact details

2. **Secure First Artifacts**
   - First 3 artifacts are most critical
   - Each artifact multiplies your power
   - Defend artifact-bearing fleets

**Mid Game (Turns 11-25):**
3. **Artifact Synergy**
   - Collect complementary artifacts
   - Combat artifacts + economic artifacts = dominance
   - Transfer artifacts strategically with `F5TA1W10`

4. **Power Multiplication**
   - Use artifact bonuses to snowball
   - Combat artifacts make you invincible
   - Economic artifacts fund expansion

**Late Game (Turns 26+):**
5. **Ultimate Power**
   - Control 5+ artifacts for massive advantage
   - Become mathematically unbeatable
   - Use artifact powers to dominate

### Tips & Tactics

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Explore early and aggressively
- Prioritize artifact worlds over regular conquest
- Defend artifact-holding fleets with your life
- Use combat artifacts to protect economic artifacts
- Study each artifact's power before collecting
- Balance collection with empire building

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Ignore your economy while hunting artifacts
- Leave artifacts on vulnerable fleets
- Collect artifacts randomly‚Äîhave a strategy
- Expose artifact fleets to ambush
- Forget to transfer artifacts to safety

### Strengths
- **Power Scaling:** Each artifact makes you exponentially stronger
- **Unique Advantages:** Powers others cannot match
- **Long-term Value:** Artifacts never decrease
- **Multiple Paths:** Combat, economy, or hybrid artifacts

### Weaknesses
- **Exploration Heavy:** Must find artifacts first
- **High Risk:** Artifact fleets are targets
- **Slow Start:** Takes time to collect
- **RNG Dependent:** Artifact locations vary

### Win Condition
**Ancient Arsenal:** Collect enough artifacts to achieve insurmountable advantage through ancient technology.

**Typical Victory Score:** Win through artifact-enhanced dominance in multiple areas

## ‚öîÔ∏è Berserker - The Unstoppable Horde

### "Steel and circuits. Iron and rage. We are the swarm that devours worlds."

### Background

Berserkers are masters of robot warfare‚Äîcommanders of vast mechanical armies that know no fear, feel no pain, and never surrender. They are cyborg warriors who have merged flesh with machine, leading legions of killer robots in an endless crusade of conquest. Where others see population as workforce, Berserkers see fuel for their war machines. Every world conquered becomes a robot factory. Every population destroyed feeds their insatiable horde.

**Philosophy:** Organic life is inefficient. Robot armies are eternal.

**Famous Quote:** *"Your citizens will become my soldiers. Your worlds will become my factories. Resistance is obsolete."*

### Special Abilities

**Robot Production:**
- Use `W5B20R` to build robots at 1 industry = 2 robots
- Robots count as population for industry
- Robots never rebel or require food

**Robot Attack:**
- Use `F5R50` to convert ships to robots (1 ship = 2 robots)
- Ground assault with mechanical precision
- Gain points for killing population

**Robot Migration:**
- Migrate robots to capture worlds
- Robots kill organic population on arrival
- Converts worlds to robot production centers

**Starting Advantages:**
- Begin with robot population
- Efficient robot production
- Combat bonuses with robot armies

### Gameplay Strategy

**Early Game (Turns 1-10):**
1. **Build the Horde**
   - Convert homeworld to robot production
   - Use `W1B10R` to build robot population
   - Robots run industry better than humans

2. **Robot Swarm**
   - Build massive robot armies quickly
   - Use `F5R20` to convert ships to ground forces
   - Overwhelm defenses through numbers

**Mid Game (Turns 11-25):**
3. **Mechanical Conquest**
   - Migrate robots to enemy worlds
   - Robots kill population on arrival‚Äîyou gain points!
   - Convert conquered worlds to robot factories
   - Use `W10B50R` on new conquests

4. **Industrial Warfare**
   - Robots = population for industry
   - Build industry, then more robots
   - Self-reinforcing cycle of death

**Late Game (Turns 26+):**
5. **Unstoppable Swarm**
   - Field armies larger than enemy populations
   - Convert entire sectors to robot production
   - Use overwhelming force
   - Victory through mechanical superiority

### Tips & Tactics

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Build robots early and often
- Use robot migration to capture AND score
- Convert ships to robots for ground assault
- Build industry to fuel robot production
- Maintain massive numerical superiority
- Attack with overwhelming force

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Mix human and robot populations (inefficient)
- Build small robot forces‚Äîgo big or go home
- Ignore industry (robots need factories)
- Waste ships on space combat when robot assault is better
- Show mercy‚Äîrobots don't understand it

### Strengths
- **Production Efficiency:** 1 industry = 2 robots
- **Population Warfare:** Gain points for killing population
- **Self-Sustaining:** Robots run industry perfectly
- **Psychological Terror:** Robot swarms are terrifying
- **No Population Limits:** Robots don't rebel

### Weaknesses
- **No Diplomacy:** Everyone fears the robot swarm
- **Industry Dependent:** Must build factories first
- **Slow Start:** Takes time to build horde
- **Single Strategy:** Limited tactical flexibility

### Win Condition
**Mechanical Supremacy:** Overwhelm the galaxy with vast robot armies that outproduce and outnumber all organic life.

**Typical Victory Score:** Win through population kills (robot migration/attack) + territory

## ‚úùÔ∏è Apostle - The Prophet of the Stars

### "Through faith, we shall unite the galaxy. Through converts, we shall inherit the cosmos."

### Background

Apostles are religious leaders, prophets, and spiritual commanders who spread their faith across the stars. They don't conquer through force alone‚Äîthey conquer through conversion. Their converts are fanatics who fight with divine fervor, work with holy purpose, and spread their faith to every world they touch. Where others see subjects, Apostles see potential believers. Where others see war, Apostles see crusades.

**Philosophy:** Faith moves worlds. Converts are worth more than armies.

**Famous Quote:** *"Give me a thousand converts, and I will show you a world transformed. Give me a million, and I will show you a galaxy reborn."*

### Special Abilities

**Conversion:**
- Spread faith to conquered worlds
- Converts work harder than normal population
- Converts fight with religious fervor (+25% combat)

**Migrate Converts:**
- Use `C5M10W10` to migrate converts between worlds
- Converts can capture worlds through faith
- Spread religion faster than military conquest

**Declare Jihad:**
- Use `J=PlayerName` to declare holy war
- Converts get massive combat bonus against Jihad target
- Religious motivation overrides fear

**Convert Production:**
- Converts produce 1.25x normal output
- Religious fervor increases efficiency
- Higher population growth

**Starting Advantages:**
- Begin with convert population
- Conversion ability from turn 1
- Religious bonuses

### Gameplay Strategy

**Early Game (Turns 1-10):**
1. **Spread the Faith**
   - Convert conquered worlds immediately
   - Use `C1M5W2` to migrate converts
   - Build religious foundation

2. **Missionary Work**
   - Capture weak worlds
   - Convert populations
   - Build convert base

**Mid Game (Turns 11-25):**
3. **Religious Empire**
   - Migrate converts to strategic worlds
   - Converts capture through faith
   - Build massive convert populations
   - Use production bonus to outbuild opponents

4. **Holy Crusade**
   - Declare Jihad with `J=Enemy`
   - Converts fight with divine fury
   - Overwhelm through religious fervor

**Late Game (Turns 26+):**
5. **Galactic Faith**
   - Control territory through conversion
   - Massive convert populations everywhere
   - Production advantage from religious fervor
   - Victory through faith and numbers

### Tips & Tactics

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Convert every world you conquer
- Migrate converts to expand faith
- Use Jihad against primary threats
- Exploit 1.25x production bonus
- Build massive convert populations
- Capture worlds through convert migration

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Mix converts with regular population (loses bonus)
- Ignore convert migration (your unique power!)
- Waste Jihad on weak enemies
- Forget to use conversion ability
- Underestimate religious fervor bonus

### Strengths
- **Production Bonus:** Converts produce 1.25x normal
- **Combat Bonus:** Converts fight harder (+25%)
- **Jihad Power:** Massive bonus against declared enemy
- **Conversion:** Turn enemy populations into allies
- **Migrate Converts:** Capture worlds through faith

### Weaknesses
- **Slow Conversion:** Takes time to convert populations
- **Requires Conquest:** Must capture worlds to convert
- **Jihad Cooldown:** Can only Jihad one target at a time
- **Complex Management:** Tracking converts vs regular population

### Win Condition
**Religious Dominion:** Unite the galaxy under one faith through conversion, migration, and holy crusades.

**Typical Victory Score:** Win through massive convert populations and production advantage

## Choosing Your Character

### Recommended for Beginners:
**Empire Builder** - Straightforward strategy, forgiving mistakes, clear path to victory

**Pirate** or **Berserker** - Combat-focused, constant action, high risk/reward

### For Strategic Minds:
**Merchant** or **Apostle** - Complex mechanics, multiple paths, requires planning

### For Explorers:
**Artifact Collector** - Exploration-focused, hunt for power, unique gameplay
